Introduction

When you use the services GripTape may provide ("the Services"), you will share some information with us. We care about your privacy and are committed to protecting it. We wrote this Privacy Policy to explain what information we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices you have for controlling, accessing, and updating your information.

By using any of our Services, you consent to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

Other products and services you may use in conjunction with our Services are subject to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy employed by those services. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you use alongside ours.

We collect information you choose to share on the services

When you use our services, we collect the information that you voluntarily choose to share here. This includes, for example, your account information (name and email address), the content of your profile (including photo, date of birth, contact info, and any other details you include), and the content of the forms, support tickets, and other material collected through engaging with our online systems.

Always use common sense with what you share through our Services, as you would on any Internet site. Don't post anything you would not want shared or saved.

Also know that when you communicate with GripTape for support or any other reason, we collect the information you voluntarily share with us (questions, feedback, suggestions, etc.)

We collect information we receive when you use our services

When you use our services, we collect information about which parts you've used and how you've used them. This helps us understand what participants like to do with our Services and helps us find ways to make the Services work better.

  • Usage information. We collect information about your activity through our Services, like when and how frequently you use them, what technology you used to access the Services (i.e., mobile or desktop computer, browser, etc.), pages you visited, number of messages you submitted, and what you clicked on.
  • Content information. We collect information about the content you provide, like its topic, hyperlinks, format, and so on.
  • Information collected by cookies and other technologies. When you login to our Services, some information is automatically collected, such as your computer's IP address and operating system, browser type, address of a referring website, time/date of your visit and site pages visited. We collect this information with tools that include Google Analytics and Cookies.
    • Google Analytics is a web analytics service offered by Google that tracks and reports website traffic. Data collected by this service is aggregated and is not personally identifiable.
    • Cookies are small files that are stored on your computer by a website to give you a unique identification. Typically, this information is also aggregated. GripTape does not require that you accept cookies, but some functionality on our Services may be disabled if you decline to accept them.

What we do with information

We use the information we collect for several purposes to ensure that the Services run efficiently, securely, and in a manner that provides participants with meaningful experiences of learning, collaboration, and support:

  • Verify your identity and protect against unauthorized use
  • Personalize and contextualize the Services for participants
  • Send participant communications, including by email and SMS
  • Store some information locally on your device, as needed to improve your experience
  • Develop, improve, operate, and protect our Services
  • Monitor and analyze trends and usage
  • Enhance the safety and security of our Services
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and other usage policies

How we share information

GripTape Services require users to affirmatively create an account with us.

We may share limited information with trusted third-party service providers who help us operate, maintain, and improve the Services. These service providers may include technology platforms that assist with data management, record keeping, communications, and program administration.

Some of these service providers use artificial intelligence (AI) to assist with program administration tasks, such as identifying duplicate records, managing program transitions, and maintaining data quality. When these tools are used, participant information (such as name, email address, and phone number) may be transmitted to and temporarily processed on the service provider's servers.

GripTape requires that all service providers processing participant information meet appropriate data protection standards. Depending on the provider, these protections are established through direct data processing agreements or provider terms of service review.

When AI-powered tools are used to process participant information:

  • Participant data will not be used to train, fine-tune, or improve general-purpose AI models
  • AI-generated recommendations are always reviewed and approved by GripTape staff before any changes are made
  • AI tools are granted access only to the specific data and operations required for their designated purpose

GripTape does not sell participant data.
